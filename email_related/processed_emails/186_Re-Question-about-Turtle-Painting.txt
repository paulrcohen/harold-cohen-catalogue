> The works at Documenta ’77 were ink on paper drawn by the Turtle.
> According to notes/correspondences from Harold, he claims they were given
> to schools. I have found no evidence of the potential names of the schools.
> Basically the same answer for the Stedlijk show also 1977. These too were
> works on paper given to schools yet to be located.
>
> As for the full color paintings that are not murals, there are 3:
>
> 1.
>
> RA26 now with Tate previously shown at they 1979 Reno Nevada show and
> also known as Reno 2 or AARON Drawing #1
> 2.
>
> RA40 sent to Gazelli also shown previously at Reno and known as Reno 1
> 3.
>
> RA46 now with Thoma not known to have been shown at a show prior to
> the Thoma acquisition – was shown on TV while in process of being painted
>
>
> As for the 1979 SFMOMA show, two things were going on. One, the Turtle
> was making drawings on paper just like Stedlijk and Documenta. Two, the
> mural behind him was executed onsite. I don’t yet know what happened to the
> mural. We might have the mural panels in the studio or we might not. I did
> open one of the folded panels a few years ago. That panel looked like a
> panel from the early 1980s – possibly the one shown at the Tate in the
> early 1980s. It was certainly not 1979. I could try opening up other panels
> but it may need to wait as the only space to accommodate the size of these
> panels is the living room and the family has visitors this week. That is,
> unless, I can figure something else out. I hope we have it as that would
> explain what happened to the SFMOMA mural. I know this maybe a whole
> ‘nother’ can of worms but there are other pre-1980 murals. They may not
> necessarily be paintings on canvas but they may exist in public space. This
> is on our list to do but not much has been down towards that yet.
> Tom

>
> If we are referring to canvas works, there were 3 executed after these
> shows. One is now at the Tate. One is with Thoma. And we sent you the 3rd
> one. We have a 4th canvas work that is black lines only in the studio – no
> coloring. I did a detailed investigation for Valentina Ravaglia of the Tate
> into their work (RA26) for their Electric Dreams which you can view here if
> interested.
>
> RA26 (Oct 1979) - A Deeper Look
> <https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1OC0hF9Pt3fRs8PBIgBQMF8shL5WZFNjIotzWs8ROll0/edit?usp=sharing>
>
> Shortly after I began working for Harold, he had me destroy a canvas work
> which may have been from that same period. But I really don’t know. It
> might have been more recent.
>
> We have a tiny collection of early non-canvas works. These are folded
> cloth works. Some are multi-panel-mural works, others a single panel. I
> hope to get to document them this year. The time period range of these
> works is not known but I rather think they are late 1970s early 1980s.
>
> If there are additional works out there, I am not aware of them. When I
> looked into the details and origins of the Tate’s RA26, I did not come
> across any information suggesting more than the known 4 canvas works were
> executed in the 1977-1979 style of AARON.
>
> Hope that helps, if you need anything else let me know,
