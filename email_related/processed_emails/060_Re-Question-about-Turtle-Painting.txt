> Hi George,
>
> *Regarding* what was made by the Turtle and your question:
> "And the only large scale drawings made by the Turtle were on paper? "
> *Short Answer *YES
>
> *Detailed Answer*
> The actual Turtle did not drawn directly on canvas. Instead, the Turtle
> made marks on photographic backdrop paper cut to size approximately 8 x 16
> feet. Some were colored by school children. The works were composed by
> AARON and drawn using the Turtle. Drawing was executed on the floor and the
> computer guided the Turtle remotely with no human intervention. The
> Turtle’s position was relayed back to the computer using ultrasonic
> navigation. I am working on a project called AARON Running. It is a
> collection of videos and animations showing AARON composing works while
> running on a computer. While my project is a in process, you can take a
> sneak peak at the link below. If you go to slide 4 you can see the a slide
> sequence animation of the Turtle drawing and thereby see how an AARON
> composition was drawn by the Turtle. I also have on slide 5 a 1980 video of
> AARON composing a Turtle Era work on screen. While it is not 1977 or 1979
> it still demonstrates how AARON would have composed similar works.
>
> https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1k6Ba5DNmJyEXRy761WeI1HzeYoz6JZ87NdS9GwJTHf0/edit?usp=sharing
>
>
> *Regarding *the murals and your question
> just so I'm clear: these are mural panels made of wood, hand painted by
> Harold, and not made by the Turtle at all?
> *Short Answer *Turtle was not used in the making of any mural
>
> *Detailed Answer*
> The murals in the studio are large cloth panels (not canvas and not wood
> panels). They are folded cloth sheets, bundled together and wrapped in
> plastic and taped up. The panels were stuck together using velcro. There
> were a dozen+ public commission murals executed onsite including UCSD,
> Stanford, Digital Equipment and others. I don’t know too much about the
> base material for these. None of these are in the studio. The Turtle was
> not used in the making of any mural. They were, like you say, hand-painted.
>
> *Regarding *the execution of the work at Gazelli and your question
> There are only three examples of the large scale drawings on canvas (like
> the one we have), but they were also painted by Harold from B+W negatives
> projected, not made by the Turtle either?
> *Short Answer *Correct
>
> *Detailed Answer*
> With respect to the execution of the 3 canvases including the one at
> Gazelli, AARON was responsible for the composition of the line drawing. Six
> negatives were made of the composition — split into upper left, middle
> upper, upper right and 3 more for the bottom. The canvas was stapled to the
> wall at his UCSD studio. The film based images were projected onto the
> canvas and hand traced. The color composition and color choices were
> decided, mixed and applied by Harold Cohen.
>
> *Summary*
> Turtle works => paper only — AARON Composed executed by Turtle
> Canvas & Mural works => non-paper — AARON Composed executed by hand
>
> If you need any thing else or further clarification let me know,
> Tom
